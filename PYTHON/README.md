CONCLUSIONS: Stroke Risk Analysis 


Employment type

Weak correlation with stroke.
Highest stroke rate among self-employed (65/819).

Place of residence

Minimal correlation.
Slightly more strokes in urban areas.

BMI

Low association with stroke.
Most strokes in overweight & obese individuals (BMI > 25).
Age plays a stronger role than BMI.

Smoking habits

Former smokers have the highest stroke incidence (~8%), followed by current smokers (~5.3%).

Average glucose level

Strongest correlation with stroke.
Diabetic patients are high-risk: 100/981 experienced a stroke.
Stroke incidence increases mainly with age, especially among diabetics.


Overall conclusion

Older individuals with hypertension, diabetes, and heart disease are most at risk.
